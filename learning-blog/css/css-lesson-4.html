Lesson 4 - The Box Model

Date: 9th October 2024


Introduction

The most important skills required to master CSS are positioning and layout. While changing the font and colour is important, being able to place things where you want them is vital. Positioning elements is not a difficult task once you understand a few core concepts. All the JavaScript skills in the world are completely meaningless if you can't place your elements in the right place.


The box model

The box model is the first CSS concept you need to understand in order to make a successful webpage. It isn't too complicated, but understanding it is important to negate frustration down the line.

Everything on a webpage is a rectangular box. These boxes can have other boxes inside of them, or can sit alongside different boxes. To gain a rough idea of how this works, you can apply an outline to every element on the page.

Even when working with circles, they still fit together as if they were rectangular boxes, not circles. Essentially, laying out a webpage and positioning its elements is deciding how you are going to nest and stack this boxes.

Complication may arise however, when manipulating the size of these boxes, and the space between them, using padding, border and margin:

  • Padding increases the space between the border of a box and the content of the box.
  • Border adds space between the margin and the padding.
  • Margin increases the space between the borders of a box and the borders of adjacent boxes.
